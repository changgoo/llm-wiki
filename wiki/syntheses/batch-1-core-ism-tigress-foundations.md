# Batch 1 Core ISM And TIGRESS Foundations

## Thesis

The first full-text batch establishes the wiki's foundation for the multiphase ISM: disk gas evolves toward statistical equilibrium when gravity, cooling, heating, turbulence, magnetic support, and feedback are allowed to interact. The key organizing principle is pressure regulation. Star formation adjusts until feedback-supplied thermal and turbulent support balances cooling, turbulent dissipation, and the vertical weight of the gas.

## Main Points

- Spiral structure can drive nonsteady shocks, phase transitions, and turbulent motions before explicit star formation feedback is included. See [[2006-kim-kim-ostriker-spiral-shock-turbulence]], [[2008-kim-kim-ostriker-spiral-shocks-thermal-instability]], and [[2010-kim-kim-ostriker-vertical-spiral-shocks]].
- Pressure-regulated star formation is validated across progressively more realistic simulations: XZ hydrodynamic models, 3D hydrodynamic models, and magnetized disks. See [[2011-kim-kim-ostriker-star-formation-regulation]], [[2013-kim-ostriker-kim-3d-star-formation-regulation]], and [[2015-kim-ostriker-magnetized-disk-star-formation]].
- Magnetic fields modify the partition of vertical support but do not erase the equilibrium relation between star formation and midplane pressure. See [[2015-kim-ostriker-magnetized-disk-star-formation]].
- [[tigress]] turns the equilibrium picture into a framework with star cluster particles, delayed supernovae, FUV heating, self-gravity, rotation, and multiphase feedback. See [[2017-kim-ostriker-tigress-framework]].
- TIGRESS connects local ISM regulation to [[galactic-winds]] by separating hot wind escape from warm fountain circulation. See [[2018-kim-ostriker-tigress-winds]].
- Spiral-arm TIGRESS models suggest spiral arms concentrate star formation without dramatically increasing the global SFR. See [[2020-kim-kim-ostriker-tigress-spiral-arms]].
- Cloud definitions matter: density-defined clouds and gravitationally bound regions are not interchangeable in a live multiphase ISM. See [[2020-mao-ostriker-kim-clouds-star-formation]].
- GalactISM extends the logic toward cosmological-model calibration, showing that galactic rotation and supernova clustering control outflow loading and can produce dynamically suppressed star formation regimes. See [[2024-jeffreson-galactism]].

## Implications

The wiki should treat [[star-formation-regulation]] and [[multiphase-ism]] as core hubs. [[stellar-feedback]] should not be filed only as energy injection; it is the mechanism that supplies the pressure needed for vertical and thermal balance. [[tigress]] is the methodological bridge from idealized equilibrium tests to realistic simulations that can predict winds and observables.

## Tensions

- Spiral shocks can drive turbulence and organize gas, but later feedback-regulated models imply stellar feedback dominates the long-term equilibrium state.
- Pressure-regulated star formation works well across many disk environments, but dynamically suppressed galaxies in [[2024-jeffreson-galactism]] show that rotation can break a simple pressure-SFR closure.
- Observational cloud virial parameters do not necessarily identify truly bound structures, complicating comparisons between simulations and molecular-cloud catalogs.
