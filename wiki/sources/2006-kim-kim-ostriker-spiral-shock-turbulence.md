# Kim, Kim, And Ostriker 2006 Spiral Shock Turbulence

## Summary

This paper studies whether galactic spiral shocks can drive turbulence in vertically stratified, self-gravitating, magnetized gas disks. In local XZ simulations of gas crossing spiral arms, the spiral potential promptly induces shocks whose fronts are curved and nonsteady rather than stationary. The resulting flapping motions inject random gas motions.

## Key Claims

- Spiral shocks in vertically stratified disks are intrinsically time-dependent because postshock vertical force balance is disrupted.
- Shock-front flapping can drive random gas motions with effective Mach numbers of a few, making spiral arms a plausible turbulence source in the ISM.
- The turbulent motions are strongest for stronger spiral forcing and are affected by magnetic fields, vertical gravity, and gas stratification.
