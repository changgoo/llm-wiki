# Kim, Kim, And Ostriker 2010 Vertically Stratified Spiral Shocks

## Summary

This paper extends the spiral-shock thermal-instability problem to vertically stratified disks. It shows that spiral shocks in stratified multiphase gas are curved, nonstationary, and subject to strong flapping motions because gas arm-crossing times and vertical oscillation periods are not commensurate.

## Key Claims

- Spiral shocks can create dense midplane slabs plus rarefied warm layers in vertically stratified disks.
- Shock flapping broadens spiral arms and can generate time-dependent arm structure.
- Thermal instability and postshock expansion regulate whether gas returns to dense or rarefied phases after passing through the arm.
- Vertical hydrostatic balance remains a useful time-averaged approximation even in the presence of spiral shocks.
