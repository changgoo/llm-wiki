# Kim And Ostriker 2018 TIGRESS Winds

## Summary

This paper analyzes vertical outflows in the solar-neighborhood TIGRESS model. It decomposes the flow by phase and shows that supernova feedback drives a superposition of hot winds and warm fountain flows.

## Key Claims

- Hot gas created by supernova shocks behaves approximately like a steady adiabatic outflow above about 1 kpc, with nearly constant mass flux and Bernoulli parameter.
- The hot gas can escape the disk, while warm gas typically lacks sufficient velocity and forms a fountain.
- For the solar-neighborhood model, the hot gas mass loading factor is around 0.1, and only a small fraction of injected supernova energy leaves the disk as hot outflow energy.
- Local phase-resolved simulations can provide boundary conditions and loading factors for larger-scale galactic wind models.
