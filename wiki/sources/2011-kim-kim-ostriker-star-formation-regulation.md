# Kim, Kim, And Ostriker 2011 Star Formation Regulation

## Summary

This paper numerically tests the thermal/dynamical equilibrium model for self-regulated star formation in diffuse atomic-dominated galactic disks. Vertically resolved simulations include differential rotation, self-gravity, vertical gravity, supernova momentum feedback, and FUV heating tied to recent star formation.

## Key Claims

- Simulated disks evolve toward vertical dynamical equilibrium: midplane thermal plus turbulent pressure balances the diffuse ISM weight.
- Thermal equilibrium also holds: midplane thermal pressure stays close to the two-phase pressure set by heating and cooling.
- Turbulent velocity dispersions remain near observed H I values and depend weakly on star formation rate.
- Star formation surface density correlates best with total midplane pressure or the pressure predicted by vertical dynamical equilibrium.
- The work calibrates feedback yield parameters for thermal and turbulent pressure per unit star formation rate.
