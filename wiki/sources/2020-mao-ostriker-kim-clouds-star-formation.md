# Mao, Ostriker, And Kim 2020 Clouds And Star Formation

## Summary

This paper identifies cloud-like structures in self-consistent TIGRESS simulations and compares cloud properties with star formation histories. It contrasts density-threshold definitions with gravitational-potential-based definitions of bound material.

## Key Claims

- Traditional virial parameters can misclassify boundedness in a structured, global ISM potential.
- High-mass density-threshold clouds can appear virialized while containing little truly bound gas.
- Bound objects in the simulations are generally dense and low mass compared with large density-defined clouds.
- Star formation correlations depend on how clouds are defined and on time delays between dense gas assembly and star formation.
