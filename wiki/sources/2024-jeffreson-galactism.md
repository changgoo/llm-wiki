# Jeffreson et al. 2024 GalactISM

## Summary

This paper presents six high-resolution chemodynamical simulations of disk and bulge-dominated galaxies designed to connect resolved star formation and outflows to subgrid modeling in cosmological simulations.

## Key Claims

- Supernova clustering, and therefore outflow mass loading, is strongly coupled to galactic rotation through the Toomre length.
- Bulge-dominated galaxies can have much lower outflow mass loading because high rotation rates suppress large cluster formation.
- The pressure-SFR relation remains promising across most simulated environments and outperforms gas surface density alone.
- Dynamically suppressed galaxies are a challenge for simple pressure-regulated prescriptions because rotation and turbulent pressure can support cold star-forming gas against collapse.
