# Kim, Ostriker, And Kim 2013 3D Star Formation Regulation

## Summary

This paper moves the pressure-regulated star formation model into full 3D hydrodynamic simulations of turbulent multiphase galactic disks with cooling, heating, self-gravity, galactic rotation, and feedback tied to recent star formation.

## Key Claims

- The 3D simulations support the same core picture as the earlier XZ models: thermal, turbulent, and vertical dynamical equilibrium are established rapidly.
- The total midplane pressure closely matches the gas weight, while thermal pressure tracks the two-phase equilibrium pressure.
- Feedback from star formation replenishes both turbulent and thermal energy losses.
- The relation between star formation rate and total midplane pressure is more direct than prescriptions based only on orbital or free-fall times.
