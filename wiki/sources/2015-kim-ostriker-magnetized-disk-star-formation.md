# Kim And Ostriker 2015 Magnetized Disk Star Formation

## Summary

This paper extends feedback-regulated galactic disk simulations to magnetized gas. It tests whether vertical equilibrium, feedback-regulated star formation, and feedback yield relations survive when MHD and different initial magnetic field strengths are included.

## Key Claims

- Quasi-steady equilibrium is reached in both hydrodynamic and MHD models, except that mean magnetic fields evolve more slowly.
- Turbulent magnetic fields saturate to similar levels across a wide range of initial magnetic strengths, consistent with small-scale dynamo amplification.
- Magnetic pressure and tension contribute to vertical support, reducing the thermal and turbulent pressure required at fixed gas weight.
- The same basic relation between kinetic midplane pressure and star formation rate remains valid, while magnetic support lowers the predicted star formation rate by roughly tens of percent relative to purely hydrodynamic models.
