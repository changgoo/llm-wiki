# Kim And Ostriker 2017 TIGRESS Framework

## Summary

This paper introduces TIGRESS, a numerical framework in Athena for local simulations of the star-forming three-phase ISM with self-gravity, sheared rotation, cooling/heating, star cluster particles, FUV heating, and supernova feedback.

## Key Claims

- TIGRESS resolves gravitational collapse into star clusters and follows delayed feedback from FUV radiation and supernovae.
- The supernova feedback algorithm switches between resolved energy injection and terminal momentum injection depending on whether the Sedov-Taylor stage is resolved.
- In a solar-neighborhood model, simulations converge for key ISM and wind quantities at resolution of about 8 pc or better.
- The fiducial model self-regulates to realistic star formation rates, multiphase volume fractions, disk scale heights, turbulent velocities, and hot gas outflows.
