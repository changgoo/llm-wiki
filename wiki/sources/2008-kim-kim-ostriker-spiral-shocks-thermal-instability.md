# Kim, Kim, And Ostriker 2008 Spiral Shocks With Thermal Instability

## Summary

This paper uses one-dimensional hydrodynamic simulations with atomic heating, cooling, and thermal conduction to study gas flow through spiral arms. Spiral shocks produce a repeating arm-interarm cycle in which warm and cold interarm gas is shocked, cools into dense arm gas, then expands into thermally unstable conditions downstream.

## Key Claims

- Spiral shocks with realistic heating and cooling naturally produce multiphase gas and phase transitions.
- Postshock expansion creates a transition zone rich in intermediate-temperature gas.
- Multiphase spiral shocks generate non-negligible random gas motions even in one-dimensional models.
- A viscous isothermal approximation can reproduce some averaged shock structure, but only after calibration against multiphase simulations.
