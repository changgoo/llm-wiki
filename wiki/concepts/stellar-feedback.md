# Stellar Feedback

## Definition

Stellar feedback is the injection of energy, momentum, mass, radiation, and metals from stars into surrounding gas through supernovae, stellar winds, photoionization, radiation fields, and related processes.

## Why It Matters

The publication corpus treats stellar feedback as the main regulator of the multiphase ISM, star formation, and galactic outflows. It is the bridge between local physics and galaxy-scale baryon cycling.
