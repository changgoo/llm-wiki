# Galactic Winds

## Definition

Galactic winds are large-scale gas outflows from galaxies. In cosmic ray feedback models, cosmic ray pressure gradients can help accelerate these outflows.

## Why It Matters

Winds regulate star formation, move metals and energy into the [[circumgalactic-medium]], and shape galaxy evolution. [[ruszkowski-pfrommer-2023-cosmic-ray-feedback]] presents cosmic rays as a candidate wind-driving agent because their pressure can build up in the interstellar medium and couple to gas over large distances.

## Supporting Evidence

- Cosmic rays are confined in galactic disks long enough for pressure gradients to develop.
- Transport speed is critical: too slow can cause excessive losses, while too fast can let cosmic rays escape before accelerating gas.
- Multiphase gas, ion-neutral damping, cosmic ray bottlenecks, magnetic fields, and radiation forces all complicate wind launching.
