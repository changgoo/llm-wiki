# AGN Feedback

## Definition

Active galactic nucleus feedback is energy, momentum, and radiation injected into surrounding gas by accreting supermassive black holes and their jets.

## Why It Matters

[[ruszkowski-pfrommer-2023-cosmic-ray-feedback]] focuses on cosmic rays accelerated in active galactic nucleus jets as a possible heating channel in massive halos, galaxy groups, and galaxy clusters. Cosmic ray protons in radio bubbles may escape into the intracluster medium and heat it through Alfvén wave excitation and damping.

## Supporting Evidence

- Cosmic ray heating may help offset radiative cooling in cool-core galaxy clusters.
- Active galactic nucleus bubbles likely contain non-thermal particle populations, but their composition and heating efficiency remain uncertain.
- Multiwavelength constraints are essential: radio, millimeter Sunyaev-Zel'dovich measurements, H-alpha, X-ray, and gamma-ray observations each test different parts of the model.
