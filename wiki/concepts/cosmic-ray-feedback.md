# Cosmic Ray Feedback

## Definition

Cosmic ray feedback is the influence of [[cosmic-rays]] on gas dynamics, heating, pressure support, star formation, winds, and galaxy or cluster evolution.

## Why It Matters

[[ruszkowski-pfrommer-2023-cosmic-ray-feedback]] frames cosmic ray feedback as a potentially major missing piece in galaxy formation models. Cosmic rays can be dynamically important because they cool slowly and couple to gas through electromagnetic and collisional processes.

## Supporting Evidence

- Cosmic rays can drive or aid [[galactic-winds]] through large-scale pressure gradients.
- Cosmic rays may provide non-thermal pressure support and heating in the [[circumgalactic-medium]].
- Cosmic rays accelerated by active galactic nucleus jets may heat intracluster gas and reduce cooling flows. See [[agn-feedback]].
- Observational constraints from radio, gamma-ray, X-ray, and line diagnostics can test feedback models.
