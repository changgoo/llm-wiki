# Cosmic Ray Transport

## Definition

Cosmic ray transport describes how [[cosmic-rays]] move through magnetized plasma by diffusion, streaming, scattering, advection, and spectral evolution.

## Why It Matters

Transport controls whether cosmic rays remain near their sources, build pressure gradients, launch winds, heat gas, or escape into the [[circumgalactic-medium]]. [[ruszkowski-pfrommer-2023-cosmic-ray-feedback]] treats transport as the central uncertainty in predictive cosmic ray feedback theory.

## Supporting Evidence

- Observed primary and secondary cosmic ray spectra can be modeled phenomenologically, but the review notes that these models are not yet fully reconciled with microphysical transport theory.
- Cosmic ray scattering depends on local plasma conditions, turbulence, wave damping, and cosmic ray driven instabilities.
- Slow transport can strengthen pressure support but worsen gamma-ray and inelastic-loss constraints; fast transport can ease loss constraints but weaken wind driving.
