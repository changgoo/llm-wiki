# Circumgalactic Medium

## Definition

The circumgalactic medium is the gas surrounding a galaxy outside its main stellar body and interstellar medium, but inside its broader halo environment.

## Why It Matters

The circumgalactic medium mediates baryon cycling between galaxies and their environments. In [[ruszkowski-pfrommer-2023-cosmic-ray-feedback]], cosmic rays may alter its pressure support, thermal state, precipitation, X-ray emission, gamma-ray constraints, and metal-line signatures.

## Supporting Evidence

- Cosmic rays transported from galaxies can provide non-thermal pressure support in the circumgalactic medium.
- Cosmic ray pressure support could make thermal gas cooler and smoother, changing absorption and emission diagnostics.
- Slow cosmic ray transport may violate gamma-ray constraints, while fast transport may weaken wind driving.
